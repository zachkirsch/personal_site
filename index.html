I'm Zach Kirsch, a junior at Tufts University, pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.A. in Math.

Right now I'm studying abroad in Milan, where I'm traveling around Europe. You can read some updates on my blog.

Tufts

Most of what I know I've learned in my courses at Tufts, where I've become interested in the theory side of computer science. I've especially enjoyed Computation Theory with Ben Hescott, Algorithms with Greg Aloupis, and Computer Systems Security with Ming Chow, where I wrote about encryption methods that are vulnerable to quantum computing.

At Tufts, I spend a lot of my time as a TA. I've always enjoyed teaching, and I like getting students excited about computer science. Last semester I TA'ed Introduction to Computer Science and Computation Theory and had a blast with both.

Projects

Outside the classroom, I've worked on a few projects. I used to participate in competitive debate, so a lot of what I've done on my own is related to that. Here are some highlights:

  • DebateHelper was the first program I ever made. It's a Microsoft Word template decked out with VBA 7 that helps collaboratively organize and consolidate research.
  • DebateRanker is a system I created for ranking debaters with the Elo and Glicko ratings systems. It pulls competition results from online sources, normalizes the data, and integrates it into the existing rankings. At last count, there were over 70,000 recorded results. DebateRanker was acquired by Victory Briefs, where it powers their national debate rankings.
