Hi!

I'm Zach Kirsch, a junior at Tufts University pursuing degrees in Computer Science and Math. Right now I'm studying abroad in Milan and traveling around Europe.

At Tufts...

I've done a lot of programming, but recently, I've become more interested in the theoretical side of computer science. There's a few courses I've especially enjoyed: Computation Theory with Ben Hescott, Algorithms with Greg Aloupis, and Computer Systems Security with Ming Chow, in which I wrote about encryption methods that are vulnerable to quantum computing. I'm planning on doing a senior thesis next year, but am still deciding on a topic.

Last semester, I TA'ed Introduction to Computer Science and Computation Theory and had a great time with both. I like the challenge of explaining new and difficult concepts to students in a way that gets them excited about computer science.

Outside of school...

I've worked on a few projects to practice what I've learned. I used to participate in competitive debate, so a lot of what I've done on my own is related to that. Here are some highlights:

  • I created DebateRanker, a system for ranking debaters with the Elo and Glicko ratings systems. It pulls competition results from online sources, normalizes the data, and integrates it into the existing rankings. At last count, there were over 70,000 recorded pairings. DebateRanker was acquired by Victory Briefs to power their national debate rankings.
  • The first program I ever wrote was DebateHelper. It's a Microsoft Word template decked out with VBA 7 that helps collaboratively organize and consolidate research. Since I released it in 2014, it's been downloaded over 2,000 times.

Over the summer...

I like to get a feel for what the working world is like for programmers. Last summer, I worked at Microsoft's Azure Customer Insights. As part of a team, I created a tool to mirror SQL stores in a graph model format to allow for relationship-based querying. This summer I'll be at Palantir Technologies in Washington, D.C.
